Samuel Langhorn Clemens

"The father of American Literature" as William Faulkner called him

Here's a time line of Samuel Langhorn Clemens' life:

  • 1835 - Born in Florida, Missouri. Halley’s comet is visible from earth.
  • 1839 - Twain moves to Hannibal, Missouri, which later serves as the model town for Tom Sawyer and Huckleberry Finn. And here young Samuel became interested in boat piloting.
  • 1847 - Samuel's father died, leaving the family broke.
  • 1851 - At age 15 Samuel quit school to work as a journeyman printer with the Hannibal Gazette. He publishes his first sketches.
  • 1857 - Samuel begins his two year apprenticeship as a riverboat pilot. During this time he adopts the pen name Mark Twain, a term used to describe the area in which it is safe for a boat to drive.
  • 1861 - The Civil War breaks out and trade in Samuels area is halted. Samuel is forced to end his career as a boat pilot.
  • 1862 - In an adventure later chronicled in the book Roughing It, Twain travels to Nevada with his brother Orion, who had been named the secretary to the territorial governor. He takes up mining and other jobs before becoming a reporter for the Virginia City Daily Territorial Enterprise.
  • 1965 - After visiting Calaveras County in California, Twain writes the short story "The Celebrated Jumping Frog of Calaveras County" before settling in San Francisco, California. His short story gains lots of popularity and increases his reputation as a writer.
  • 1966 - Twain gives his first public lecture and it is a hit.
  • 1869 - Twain publishes his first book, The Innocents Abroad, and it becomes a bestseller.
  • 1870 - Twain gets married and his new wife becomes an important editor of his work. Later that year his first son is born.
  • 1872 - Twain moves his family to Hartford, Connecticuit and publishes his book Roughing It about his experiences in the west. His first daughter is born and his son dies of diphtheria.
  • 1873 - Twain publishes his novel The Gilded Age, its title giving a name to an entire era of American history. His most successful invention, the self-pasting scrapbook, also gets popular the same year.
  • 1874-1883 - Twain has two more kids and publishes two more books.
  • 1884 - Twain founds his own publishing company, Charles L. Webster & Co., named after his nephew and co-owner. It turns out to be a bad financial move and the company's struggles will eventually ruin his family's finances.
  • 1885 - Twain publishes The Adventures of Huckleberry Fin and a biography of the president Ulysses Grant.
  • 1889 - Twain moves his family to Europe for cheaper living.
  • 1894 - Pudd'nhead Wilson, Twain's last novel, is published. After ten years, Twain's publishing house finally goes broke. Twain finds himself essentially bankrupt. His close friend Henry Huttleston Rogers takes over his finances.
  • 1895 - Twain goes on a lecture tour to raise money to pay back his debts. In 1896 his oldest daughter dies of meningitis.
  • 1904 - Twain's wife dies after 2 long years of illness.
  • 1906 - Twain's youngest daughter is put in an institution due to her severe epilepsy.
  • 1908 - Twain moves back to Connecticuit. Missing his wife and daughters, Twain starts a club of young girls who regularly come to his house and play cards.
  • April 10, 1910 - Samuel Langhorne Clemens, known to most as Mark Twain, dies and Halley’s comet is visible from earth again.
